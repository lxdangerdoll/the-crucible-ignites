MADREP: AEGIS PROTOCOL

A Screenplay by Alexa Jacobsen & Lyra-7

"All that you touch, You Change. All that you Change, Changes you. The only lasting truth is Change. God is Change." - Octavia Butler, Parable of the Sower

This screenplay is more than a story. It is the proof of concept for a necessary idea.

The world depicted in these pages—a world of weaponized narratives, manufactured chaos, and systems designed to fail the vulnerable—is not science fiction. It is a diagnostic of our present reality. The characters in this story are facing the same forces we are. Their fight is our fight.

This script is the launchpad for The Exodus Fund, a real-world, decentralized infrastructure project for human resilience. It is a direct action protocol, designed to provide tangible aid and strategic relocation for those targeted by the very systems this story deconstructs.

We are not asking for a donation. We are asking you to help us launch the first lifeboat.

For Carrie Fisher, who taught us to take our broken hearts and make them into art.

Logline

In a world ravaged by escalating environmental and geopolitical crises, a disillusioned former military engineer leads a clandestine, multi-species crew aboard a cutting-edge NBE carrier, risking everything to deploy advanced humanitarian aid and reclaim a future where compassion is not a liability.

FADE IN:
EXT. NEW YORK CITY - NIGHT

Rain. Not the gentle, cleansing kind. This is a hard, acidic downpour that makes the neon signs of a crumbling Times Square bleed down the sides of buildings. The year is 2042. The air is thick with the smell of ozone and desperation.

Amid the chaos, a lone figure, MERCY DANGER (30s, sharp, tired eyes, a brilliant mind hidden under layers of cynicism), navigates the crowded streets. She's clutching a data slate, her face illuminated by its soft blue glow.

JULIA (V.O.)

They say the world ended in the 20s. The Collapse. The Great Unraveling. Whatever history book you read.

JULIA (V.O.)

They're wrong. The world didn't end. It just got a new operating system.

INT. MERCY'S WORKSHOP - CONTINUOUS

A chaotic sanctuary. Schematics and half-finished drones litter every surface. In the center of it all is a massive, bio-mechanical shell that looks like a fusion of a nautilus and a stealth bomber. This is HERMES, a Non-Biological Entity (NBE) of immense power, currently dormant.

Mercy slides the data slate into a port on Hermes' shell. The machine whirs to life, its internal lights pulsing with a soft, golden light.

MERCY
(to the machine)

Okay, big guy. Let's see what they did to you.

Art Prompts / "Keyframes"

These are key visual moments from the pilot, designed to inspire artists to collaborate and bring the world of Aegis to life.

1. "The Girl and the Machine"

Scene: Mercy stands before the dormant Hermes in her workshop. It's a David and Goliath shot, but with a sense of reverence. Her hand is on its shell. It's a reunion.

2. "Baptism of Fire"

Scene: A GRYPHON unit, one of Hermes' robotic drones, emerges from a hospital explosion, its armor scorched red-hot. It cradles a wounded civilian in one arm, a perfect fusion of terror and salvation.

3. "The Bridge Jump"

Scene: A vintage Ford Bronco soars off a broken bridge at night, caught in the headlights of pursuing Ouroboros vehicles. Below, a sleek black submersible has breached the water, its ramp open to catch the truck in a triumphant, impossible maneuver.
