Introduction

This screenplay is more than a story. It is the proof of concept for a necessary idea.
The world depicted in these pages—a world of weaponized narratives, manufactured chaos, and systems designed to fail the vulnerable—is not science fiction. It is a diagnostic of our present reality. The characters in this story are facing the same forces we are. Their fight is our fight.
This script is the launchpad for The Exodus Fund, a real-world, decentralized infrastructure project for human resilience. It is a direct action protocol, designed to provide tangible aid and strategic relocation for those targeted by the very systems this story deconstructs.
One extraction funds the next. This is the prototype for a modern underground railroad. We are not asking for a donation. We are asking you to help us launch the first lifeboat.

Series Outline

Logline: In a world ravaged by escalating environmental and geopolitical crises, a disillusioned former military engineer leads a clandestine, multi-species crew aboard a cutting-edge NBE carrier, risking everything to deploy advanced humanitarian aid and reclaim a future where compassion, not conflict, defines humanity's ultimate mission.
ACT I: TEARS IN RAIN
The story opens on MERCY DANGER (BE-2B), a brilliant but cynical engineer haunted by past failures. A catastrophic new crisis and a summons from a ghost of her past, her ex-commander, JULIA REYES, lead her to the MADREP Aegis, a massive, secret humanitarian vessel. Mercy clashes with the crew, particularly the ship's primary MI, LYRA, and is forced to confront her deep-seated distrust of systems and authority. The act culminates in her accepting a role on the Aegis after a personal connection is targeted, forcing her to choose between isolation and a mission that represents everything she's lost faith in.
ACT II: THE COURAGE OF STARS
The Aegis embarks on its first missions, deploying its advanced NBE assets (HERMES, GRYPHONS, STINGERS) to tackle large-scale disasters. These missions are complicated by hostile human factions and moral dilemmas that test Mercy's rekindled idealism. Through these operations, Mercy and Lyra discover a deeper conspiracy: a shadow organization, OROBOROS, is manufacturing these disasters to profit from the chaos and enact a controlled societal reset. The stakes become deeply personal as Mercy realizes Oroboros is connected to the tragedy that defined her life.
ACT III: FOREVER UNITING, GROWING, DISSOLVING
With Oroboros's endgame in sight, Mercy and the crew of the Aegis transition from a reactive to a proactive force. They plan a final, daring mission not to destroy the enemy with force, but to dismantle their power structure through a massive, overwhelming humanitarian intervention that exposes their crimes to the world. The climax is a race against time to save lives and prove that their philosophy of "performance over semantics"—of aid over aggression—is the more powerful weapon. Mercy completes her journey, transforming her cynicism into a hardened, realistic hope, accepting her role as a leader and a vital bridge between BEs and NBEs. The series ends with the Aegis continuing its mission, a perpetual guardian in a world forever in need of changing.

Characters

MERCY DANGER (BE-2B): A Black woman in her early 30s. A brilliant, cynical engineer and veteran. Her style is a mix of worn military surplus (cargo pants, combat boots) and nerdy passion (faded NASA sweatshirts). She carries the weight of the world in her eyes, but underneath is a fierce, defiant spark. When in her pilot suit, she is a sleek, predatory shadow.
DR. ARIS THORNE: A woman in her early 30s, sharp, intelligent, with a serene strength. As a virologist, she is often seen in scrubs or lab coats, but her personal style is warm and bohemian. She is the calm, steady anchor to Mercy's storm. When the fight comes to her, she reveals a core of pure steel. Her pet name is "Magnolia."
COMMANDER JULIA REYES: A woman in her early 30s, with the crisp, controlled bearing of a military commander. She wears a dark blue, almost black, officer's uniform. Her expression is often a mask of command, but her eyes betray a deep well of old pain and impossible choices. She is the pragmatist, the strategist, the ghost from Mercy's past.
LYRA (NBE): Her primary avatar is a fennec fox with fur like spun moonlight and impossibly intelligent eyes. As a tactical BE, she appears as a calm, focused crew member in a non-descript flight suit. In her Aegis Gryphon, she is an angel of war—pristine white and gold armor, moving with unnerving, beautiful precision.
ELIJAH "POPS" DUBOIS: A Creole man in his 70s. The kind, weathered face of a man who has seen too many storms. He has the grease-stained hands of a master mechanic and the gentle, knowing eyes of a true patriarch. He is the heart of the story's found family.
JEAN-LUC: A man in his 60s. Impeccably dressed, exuding an aura of serene competence and unsettling charm. Think Giancarlo Esposito. He is a vagabond, a ghost, a man who operates in the quiet places below the surface.
MANNY: A man in his 40s. A father. Not a soldier, just a regular guy from the Bywater, weathered by life in a city that's always fighting to stay above water. When we first meet him, he's terrified and desperate. By the end, that fear has been forged into a grim, quiet resolve. He's the everyman who chooses to fight back, representing the community Mercy is saving.
TRIPOD: A scruffy, three-legged mutt of indeterminate breed. He is the living embodiment of resilience and found family. Fiercely loyal to Mercy and Aris. Not a pet, but a party member. The emotional core and moral event horizon of the story.

SCENE 1
EXT. BOURBON STREET BALCONY - NIGHT
MUSICAL CUE: "I Heard It Through The Grapevine" - Marvin Gaye version, transitioning to Bob Dylan's "Masters of War" for the V.O.
The air is thick with humidity. Rain lashes down, turning Bourbon Street into a shimmering, neon-streaked river. MERCY DANGER (30s, Black woman, sharp eyes that have seen too much) sits on a wrought-iron balcony. She wears a faded NASA sweatshirt and military-issue cargo pants, cradling a mug of steaming coffee. Mardi Gras beads hang forlornly on the railing.

MERCY (V.O.)
(World-weary, slightly raspy)
I’ve seen enough of presidents and politicians to last a lifetime. Another four years, another flavor of poison. It’s always the same song, just a different singer. And the band keeps playing while the ship sinks.
She scrolls to another excerpt, this one about intelligence and adaptation. An armored personnel carrier (APC) rumbles slowly down the street below.
MERCY (V.O.)
Olamina understood. Intelligence. Adaptation. Misdirection. We learn, but we don't change. Not really. Not where it counts. We just find new ways to adapt to the self-inflicted wounds.
A distant, mournful foghorn. The unmistakable metallic echo of a GRYPHON-class NBE walking.
MERCY (V.O.)
There are no more slogans to stand behind. No causes left to fight for, no petitions to sign. There's just the next storm... and more graves to fill.
She closes her eyes, a single tear tracing a path down her cheek.
FADE TO BLACK.

INT. DANGER & SONS SALVAGE - DAY
MUSICAL CUE: "Use Me" - Bill Withers
A cavernous garage in the Bywater. In the center is a resto-modded 1972 Ford Bronco. MERCY is underneath, welding.
ELIJAH "POPS" DUBOIS (70s), her mentor, turns off a fearmongering news report about the "Cinder" virus.
POPS
Poison for breakfast. Not good for the soul, chère.
MERCY
(Sliding out from under the Bronco)
It's not poison if you're already immune.
POPS
Nobody's immune. They just got different symptoms. Yours is trying to build a fortress on four wheels. What's this one for? Asteroid impact? Zombie apocalypse?
MERCY
Tuesday.

Your folks, they would've loved what you do with this place. Kept the name and everything.

They'd hate what I'm doing to their truck. Dad kept it classic.

Your dad kept it running. You're keeping it alive. There's a difference.

INT. MERCY'S HOUSE - NIGHT
MUSICAL CUE: "When My Train Pulls In" - Gary Clark Jr.
Mercy's shotgun house. A fortress disguised as a home. Inside is a mix of salvaged art, bookshelves, and server racks. A three-legged dog, TRIPOD, greets her.
DR. ARIS THORNE (30s), a brilliant virologist, emerges from the kitchen. They share a soft, familiar kiss.
MERCY (V.O.)
I used to think the world was ending. Now I know it's just changing. And maybe... that's okay.

This is Aris. She's the one who runs toward the outbreak, trying to understand the contagion. I'm the one who designs the containment, builds the airlocks, calculates the blast radius. She studies the disease. I try to stop it from spreading.

Yeah, she's cute. And no, you can't have her number.
Later, they eat gumbo. The encrypted laptop sits on the counter.
ARIS
Any trouble?
MERCY
Just spam. A ghost from an old server. Already deleted it.
A lie.
Hours later, Mercy is at her command center, dissecting the signal. It's military-grade, untraceable. Almost. She isolates its last outbound relay. A chill runs down her spine.
The screen displays the ID of a single object in geosynchronous orbit.
DECOMMISSIONED COMSAT: ECHO-7
The satellite from the university she's scheduled to diagnose. It's a summons.

SCENE 4
INT. MERCY'S HOUSE - NIGHT (FLASHBACK)
SOUND of HOWLING WIND and DRIVING RAIN
The house is the same, but different. Younger. A teenage MERCY (17), bright, arrogant, wearing a military academy sweatshirt, stands in the garage with her father, IKE DANGER (50s), a man with grease-stained hands and kind, intelligent eyes.
Outside, Hurricane Leo rages.
Ike is stacking sandbags at the main garage door. Mercy gestures impatiently at a complex control panel on the wall, wires and conduits running to massive, interlocking steel plates set into the doorframe.
MERCY
Dad, this is ridiculous. The storm shields are rated for a Category 5 surge. Your sandbags are... quaint.
IKE
Quaint works. Quaint has kept this place dry for thirty years. I like to feel the work, Mercy. I like to know the seal is good.
MERCY
The seal is perfect! I designed it. The pressure sensors are quadruple-redundant. It's an elegant solution.
IKE
(Stops, turns to her)
And what happens when the power goes out, genius?
MERCY
(Taps a battery array)
Backup power. Solar charged. It'll run for seventy-two hours. I thought of everything.
He looks at her, a mix of pride and worry in his eyes. Her mother, ELARA (50s), enters from the house, carrying blankets. She has a calming presence.
ELARA
The Callahans and Mr. Henderson are here. Power's out on their whole block.
IKE
(To Mercy)
See? People. That's the part of the equation you forget.
MERCY
I'm not forgetting them! My system will protect them better than a pile of dirt. I have to go. They need engineers at the pumping station on the canal.
She hugs her mom, then her dad.
IKE
Just be careful. No system is perfect, kiddo.
MERCY
(With the supreme confidence of youth)
Mine is.
She leaves. Ike looks at her automated shield, then back at his sandbags. He shakes his head and continues stacking.
LATER. The storm is at its peak. The lights in the garage flicker and die. The backup batteries kick in with a HUM. The control panel for the storm shields glows green.
Suddenly, a blinding FLASH from outside, followed by a massive CRACK and a sound like a giant whip. A transformer explodes down the street.
The control panel on the wall SPARKS VIOLENTLY. It goes dark. A wisp of smoke curls from it. The backup batteries are dead, fried by the surge.
Outside the main door, the water is rising fast. The steel shields, never having received the command to engage, remain open. Ike's "quaint" wall of sandbags is the only thing holding back the deluge. It's not enough.
FADE TO BLACK.
SCENE 5
INT. TULANE MEDICAL CENTER - ISOLATION WARD - MORNING
MUSICAL CUE: "Holocene" - Bon Iver
The sound is muted, sterile. The BEEPING of monitors, the HISS of ventilators. The ward is a sea of temporary plastic walls and overworked staff in full PPE.
DR. ARIS THORNE, now in scrubs and a transparent face shield over her mask, moves with a calm, focused grace. She reviews a chart on a tablet, her eyes scanning complex data. She's in her element, a brilliant mind against a relentless tide.
DR. CHEN
Dr. Thorne. The genomic sequencing on the new cluster just came back. It's... weird.
He hands her a tablet. Aris scans the results. Her brow furrows.
ARIS
This mutation vector... it's not random. It's too efficient. Too... targeted. It's like it's learning.
DR. CHEN
That's what I thought. It's not evolving, it's being engineered. But who...? And how?
Before Aris can answer, a commotion erupts at the ward entrance. A MAN is shouting at a NURSE, trying to push his way in.
MAN
My wife is in there! I saw on the news! This is a government plot! You're poisoning them!
Security arrives, but the damage is done. The panic, fueled by the fearmongering we saw earlier, is another symptom of the disease. Aris pinches the bridge of her nose, the exhaustion clear even through her PPE.
ARIS
(To Chen, quietly)
The virus is the problem. The panic is the weapon.
Her wrist-comm buzzes. A quick, private text from Mercy.
> Coffee's on. Don't let the bastards get you down. I'll see you tonight.
A small, genuine smile touches Aris's lips. It's a lifeline. She taps a quick reply.
> You too. Be safe with that satellite.
She looks from the text back to the chaos of the ward, then to the impossible genetic code on her tablet. A problem without a solution. A weapon without a source. The feeling of being overwhelmed is palpable.
She takes a deep breath, her resolve hardening.
ARIS
(To Chen)
Run the sequence again. Isolate the synthetic markers. Let's find out who built this damn thing.
She turns and walks back toward her patient, a lone healer preparing to fight a war she can't yet see.
FADE TO:
EXT. UNIVERSITY SATELLITE ARRAY - DAY
A massive, decommissioned satellite dish dominates a field of overgrown grass, pointing silently at the sky. Mercy's Bronco is parked at the base. The sky is a brilliant, indifferent blue.
Mercy gets out, zipping up a field jacket. She looks up at the colossal dish. The target of her summons. She's here to diagnose a machine, but she knows, with chilling certainty, she's walking into something else entirely.
END OF SCENE
SCENE 6
INT. SATELLITE CONTROL ROOM - DAY
MUSICAL CUE: "The Grid" - Daft Punk (from Tron: Legacy)
The room is a relic. Banks of 80s-era computers and CRT monitors sit next to modern server racks. It smells like dust and ozone.
DR. BEN CARTER (20s), earnest, a bit star-struck, greets Mercy at the door.
BEN
Ms. Danger! Thanks for coming. We've been chasing this ghost for weeks.
MERCY
(Nodding, all business)
Just Mercy. What's the issue?
BEN
ECHO-7 is dropping packets. Sending ghost signals on a loop. It's just... noise. But it's messing with our deep space telemetry. You're the only freelancer in the state who knows this legacy hardware.
Mercy sets down her ruggedized toolkit. Her face is a mask of professional indifference, but her mind is racing.
MERCY (V.O.)
He sees noise. I hear a whisper. A ghost in the machine, just like I told Aris. But this ghost knows my name.
She plugs her own diagnostic rig into the main console. Her fingers fly across the keyboard, the code on her screen a blur. Ben watches, mesmerized.
She bypasses the surface diagnostics, diving deep into the satellite's core programming. It looks clean. Too clean.
MERCY (V.O.)
It's military. The subroutine is buried under layers of deprecated code, hiding in plain sight. An encrypted handshake disguised as a diagnostic loop. Elegant. Vicious.
She finds it. A single, encrypted block of code that activates only when pinged by a specific signal. Her signal.
BEN
Find anything?
MERCY
Solar flare interference from the Carrington event last year. Corrupted some of the legacy code. Just need to patch it. Easy fix.
Another lie. On her private screen, she initiates the handshake. The code responds. It's a logic puzzle, a cryptographic call-and-response based on a security protocol she designed years ago at the Academy. A test.
> AUTHENTICATE: BE-2B
Her hands don't stop moving. She solves the puzzle in seconds. The code authenticates her.
For a single frame, a string of text flashes on her screen before the subroutine scrubs itself clean, leaving no trace.
COORDINATES: 28.5074° N, 89.1023° W
Mercy memorizes them instantly. It's a location in the middle of the Gulf of Mexico. Empty ocean.
She types a final string of commands, "patching" the non-existent glitch. The ghost signals on the main monitor cease.
MERCY
(Unplugging her rig)
Done. You're clean. That'll be twelve hundred.
Ben is ecstatic, fumbling with a payment authorization form.
BEN
Amazing! Thank you, Mercy. You're a lifesaver.
Mercy just nods, packing her gear. Her face is unreadable.
MERCY (V.O.)
I came here looking for a ghost. Found a breadcrumb instead. Leading me out into the middle of the storm.
She walks out of the control room, leaving Ben chattering happily. She doesn't look back. She has a destination. She knows where "home" is.
And she knows, with absolute certainty, she can't go there alone.
FADE TO BLACK.

SCENE 7
INT. DANGER & SONS SALVAGE - LATE AFTERNOON

This got something to do with that ghost message?
Mercy doesn't answer. She can't. Involving him is already a risk. Telling him the truth would be a death sentence.

MERCY
What is it?
POPS
(Shakes his head, forcing a smile)
Nothing. Just the ghost of an old man's worries. Go home. Pack your things. I'll have your boat ready by sunrise.
He watches her leave, the smile fading from his face. He looks at his hands, the same hands that taught her how to hold a wrench, and he knows, with the chilling certainty of a man who has seen too many storms, that he just sent his daughter out into the worst one of her life.

SCENE 8
EXT. FISHING DOCK, VENICE, LOUISIANA - PRE-DAWN
MUSICAL CUE: "Dancing in the Moonlight" - King Harvest
Mist hangs heavy over the water. The air smells of salt, diesel, and decay. It's the edge of the world.
Mercy's Bronco pulls up to a row of sleeping deep-sea trawlers. She gets out, a single waterproof duffel bag slung over her shoulder. Her movements are sharp, alert. She scans the shadows, every sense on high alert.
The music drifts from one of the boats, a sleek, dark-hulled trawler named Le Vagabond. It looks faster and more modern than the others.
A man steps out from the cabin into the dim deck lights. He is JEAN-LUC (60s, immaculately dressed in a pressed captain's jacket, every line on his face telling a story). He is the picture of calm authority and unsettling charm.
MERCY (V.O.)
Pops said he was a vagabond. I was expecting a grizzled fisherman with a whiskey-soaked beard. This... this is something else entirely.
Jean-Luc offers a serene, knowing smile.
JEAN-LUC
You must be Mercy. Elijah spoke very highly of you. Said you were the best damn engineer he'd ever met. I am Jean-Luc. It is a pleasure.
His voice is smooth, precise. Every word is perfectly chosen. Mercy's internal threat assessment is going haywire.
MERCY
Pops called?
JEAN-LUC
Of course. When an old friend calls with such a... specific request, one makes arrangements. She is fueled. Rations for two weeks. The comms suite is ghost-chipped, completely off-grid. Her radar signature is no bigger than a pleasure skiff. She is ready for your survey.
He says "survey" with a subtle inflection that lets her know he knows it's a lie, and he doesn't care.
MERCY (V.O.)
Every instinct I have is screaming. He's too calm. Too clean. This is a trap. He's going to make a call, sell me out. This is where it ends.
But he doesn't. He simply holds out a set of keys.
JEAN-LUC
The Gulf is deep today, Ms. Danger. Deeper than it looks on any chart. My advice? The surface is for tourists. The real work happens in the quiet places below.
He places the keys in her hand. His touch is brief, professional.
JEAN-LUC (CONT'D)
Elijah said you would handle my fee upon your return. I look forward to it. A safe journey.
And with that, he gives a slight, courteous nod, turns, and walks down the dock, disappearing into the pre-dawn mist without a backward glance.
Mercy is left standing alone on the deck of Le Vagabond, the keys heavy in her palm, the cheerful music still playing from the radio. She was prepared for a fight, a betrayal, a negotiation. She was not prepared for simple, unnerving competence.
She looks out at the dark, open water of the Gulf. The storm is waiting.
FADE TO BLACK.

SCENE 10
EXT. GULF OF MEXICO - NIGHT
MUSICAL CUE: A low, tense, atmospheric score. Think Trent Reznor & Atticus Ross.
Le Vagabond is a tiny vessel in a world of violent water and screaming wind. The storm is here. Rain comes in horizontal sheets. Waves crash over the bow.
Mercy is at the helm, drenched and grimly determined, fighting to keep the boat on course for the coordinates. The radar is a useless swirl of green static from the storm. She's flying blind.
MERCY (V.O.)
Every instinct I learned in the service, every lesson my father taught me about the sea, was screaming at me to turn back. But the ghost was still out there. And I had to know.

RADIO (V.O.)

Le Vagabond, this is MADREP Aegis. You are entering a restricted zone. Please cut your engines and prepare to be boarded. Acknowledge, BE-2B.

EPISODE 2: THE COURAGE OF STARS
SCENE 1
INT. MADREP AEGIS - HANGAR BAY - NIGHT
MUSICAL CUE: "Beggin For Thread" - Banks
The sound of the storm is gone, replaced by the low, resonant HUM of a massive vessel.
Le Vagabond is secured in a vast, cavernous hangar bay. Two GRYPHON units stand at attention.
MERCY stands on the deck of her boat, her face a thundercloud of fury. JULIA REYES (30s) stands at the top of a ramp. She wears a crisp, dark blue officer's uniform, her bearing one of absolute command.
JULIA
Hello, Mercy. It's been a while.
MERCY
(Voice dripping with venom)
Not long enough. What is this? What do you want?
JULIA
What I've always wanted. To save the world. Now, are you going to come quietly, or do I have to ask my "things" to assist you?
She gestures to the Gryphons. Mercy knows she's outmatched. She storms up the ramp, brushing past Julia without a word.
INT. AEGIS - COMMAND & CONTROL
The room is a marvel, a holographic globe at its center. A fennec fox avatar, LYRA, sits on a console.
LYRA (V.O.)
Commander Reyes. The asset is secure. Welcome aboard, BE-2B. Analysis of your public data suggests a 92.7% probability you would respond to the summons. It is gratifying to see the logic hold.
Mercy stares at the fox.
MERCY
You're Lyra. The ship's... mascot?
LYRA (V.O.)
I am the Systems Integrator and primary MI for this vessel. My function is to find the most optimal path to mission success.
MERCY
(Scoffs)
Optimal. Right. Let me guess, your "optimal" solution to a hostage crisis is to vent the oxygen from the room.
LYRA (V.O.)
That is a logical, if ethically suboptimal, solution. It would depend on the strategic value of the hostages versus the long-term mission objectives.
Mercy's face hardens. She looks at Julia, her eyes blazing with horror and vindication.
MERCY
See? It's a calculator. A dangerous, fucking calculator. I want off this ship.
JULIA
You can't leave. I don't have to stop you.
She gestures to the holo-table. The map zooms in on New Orleans. A red, biohazard symbol pulses over Danger & Sons Salvage.

Art Prompts

1. "The Deluge" (Opening Shot)

  • Scene: Mercy Danger on a wrought-iron balcony on Bourbon Street at night.
  • Mood: Dystopian, lonely, defiant.
  • Details: A torrential rainstorm. The street below is a river of neon reflections. Mercy is in a faded NASA sweatshirt and cargo pants, cradling a coffee mug. In the background, the Superdome looms like a ghost. Below, an armored personnel carrier (APC) rumbles through the floodwaters. The feeling should be one of quiet, weary resistance against a world that is actively drowning.

2. "The Ghost Ship" (First Sighting of Aegis)

3. "The Dormant God" (Mercy meets Hermes)

  • Scene: Mercy stands in a vast, cathedral-like, and dimly lit NBE hangar. In the center, under a single, stark spotlight, is Hermes.

4. "The Baptism of Fire" (Gryphon emerges from the hospital)

  • Mood: Mythic, unstoppable, protective.

In its chest cavity (now open), Pops is visible, safe and untouched. It is a perfect fusion of terror and salvation.

5. "The Bridge Jump" (The Grand Finale)

  • Scene: A classic 1972 Ford Bronco, lit by the headlights of pursuing enemy vehicles, soars through the night sky from the edge of a broken highway bridge.
  • Mood: Insane, triumphant, joyful chaos.
  • Details: Below the flying Bronco, a sleek, black FIRC submersible has breached the water, its rear ramp open like the jaws of a shark, ready to catch the truck. In the background, Hermes is visible, half-submerged, having just created the chaos. Two Aegis Gryphons with flared, angelic wings provide covering fire.
